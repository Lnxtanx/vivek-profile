DIY JCB from Cardboard

Materials Required:

  • Cardboard sheets
  • Glue gun or strong adhesive
  • Scissors or a craft knife
  • Wooden skewers or dowels (for joints and axles)
  • Rubber bands (for flexible movement)
  • Paints or markers (for coloring)
  • Ruler and pencil (for measurements)
  • Small wheels (optional for added mobility)

Steps to Make the JCB:

  1. Design and Planning: Sketch the parts of the JCB on cardboard, including the base, arm, cabin, and bucket.
  2. Cutting the Cardboard: Use a craft knife to cut out the required shapes with precision.
  3. Building the Base: Assemble the base and attach wheels or tracks for mobility.
  4. Constructing the Arm: Create a movable arm using skewers and rubber bands for flexibility. Attach the bucket at the end.
  5. Making the Cabin: Assemble the cabin and fix it to the chassis.
  6. Assembling the Parts: Attach the arm to the base with pivot joints and ensure all parts are secure.
  7. Adding Finishing Touches: Paint the model in yellow and black, and add detailed decorations.

Tips for Success:

  • Be precise with measurements for a neat model.
  • Use thicker cardboard for structural parts like the arm and base.
  • Reinforce joints with extra glue or layers of cardboard if necessary.
